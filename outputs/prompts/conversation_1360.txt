User: Top causes of red eye in immunocompromised patients
Assistant: In immunocompromised patients, red eye can be caused by various factors including infections (bacterial, viral, fungal), inflammation, or trauma. Common infectious causes include Herpes Simplex Keratitis, Cytomegalovirus Retinitis, and fungal keratitis. Non-infectious causes may include dry eye syndrome or uveitis.
User: Fungal keratitis most common species
Assistant: Fungal keratitis is often caused by filamentous fungi like Fusarium and Aspergillus species, and also yeast-like fungi such as Candida species. The prevalence can vary based on geographic location and environmental factors.
User: Treatment options for Fusarium keratitis
Assistant: Fusarium keratitis is typically treated with topical antifungal agents such as natamycin 5% drops, which is considered first-line therapy. Amphotericin B and voriconazole may also be used in certain cases. Oral antifungal agents can be considered for severe infections. Treatment duration can be several weeks, depending on response.
User: Side effects of natamycin eye drops
Assistant: Common side effects of natamycin eye drops include eye irritation, discomfort, redness, blurred vision, tearing, or allergic reactions. It's important to monitor the patient for any adverse effects during treatment.
User: In case of natamycin resistance, alternative treatments?
Assistant: If a fungal keratitis case is resistant to natamycin, alternative treatments include topical voriconazole 1% drops or amphotericin B 0.15% drops. Systemic antifungal agents, such as oral voriconazole or itraconazole, can be considered in conjunction with topical therapy for severe or unresponsive cases.
