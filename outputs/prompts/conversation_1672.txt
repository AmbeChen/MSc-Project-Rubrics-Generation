User: Does a TIA always cause permanent damage?
Assistant: No, a transient ischemic attack (TIA) does not typically cause permanent damage, as symptoms usually resolve within minutes to hours. However, it can be a warning sign of a future stroke and prompt evaluation is important.
User: Is it okay to drive after a TIA?
Assistant: Driving after a TIA depends on how quickly you recover and whether you’ve had recurrent symptoms. Many guidelines suggest waiting a certain period before resuming driving, but the details can vary, so you should ask your doctor when it’s safe to drive again and whether any local regulations apply.
