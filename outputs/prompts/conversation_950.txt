Assistant: A Mediterranean-style eating plan typically focuses on whole grains, fruits, vegetables, legumes, nuts, olive oil, and moderate amounts of fish and poultry. It has been linked to heart health in various studies. Here are some key points:

### 1. **Core Foods**
- **Fruits and Vegetables**: Provide vitamins, minerals, and fiber.
- **Whole Grains**: Help with steady energy levels and support cardiovascular wellness.
- **Healthy Fats**: Emphasize olive oil and nuts, which contain beneficial unsaturated fats.

### 2. **Evidence for Heart Benefits**
- **Reduced Cardiovascular Risk**: Multiple studies suggest lower rates of heart disease among people following a Mediterranean eating pattern.
- **Cholesterol Management**: May help maintain healthy cholesterol levels.
- **Blood Pressure**: Often associated with improved blood pressure control.

### 3. **Lifestyle Integration**
- **Balanced Approach**: Encourages portion control and a variety of nutrient-rich foods.
- **Long-Term Sustainability**: Flexible food choices that can be adapted to personal tastes.

### 4. **General Advice**
- **Stay Active**: Regular physical activity also contributes to heart health.
- **Moderation**: Incorporate moderate servings of lean protein, dairy, and healthy fats.
