Assistant: **Gonorrhea** is a sexually transmitted infection (STI) caused by the bacterium **Neisseria gonorrhoeae**. Below is an overview of its key features:

### **Transmission**
- Primarily spreads through unprotected vaginal, anal, or oral intercourse
- Can also be passed from mother to baby during childbirth

### **Symptoms**
- Men: Burning sensation during urination, white/yellow/green discharge
- Women: Increased vaginal discharge, pain or burning with urination, possible bleeding between periods
- Many infections are asymptomatic, especially in women

### **Potential Complications**
- Pelvic inflammatory disease (PID) in women, which can lead to infertility or ectopic pregnancy
- Epididymitis in men, causing testicular pain and potentially affecting fertility
- Disseminated gonococcal infection (rare, but serious)

### **Testing**
- Usually involves a swab from the cervix, urethra, throat, or rectum
- Urine tests are sometimes used

Assistant: **Neisseria gonorrhoeae** is the **bacterium** responsible for gonorrhea. Below are some important points:

### **Characteristics**
- Gram-negative diplococcus
- Primarily infects mucous membranes of the reproductive tract, mouth, throat, eyes, and rectum

### **How Infection Develops**
- Bacteria adhere to and invade mucosal cells
- Can evade certain immune defenses, leading to persistent infection

### **Testing Methods**
- Nucleic acid amplification tests (NAATs) from swabs or urine samples are highly accurate
- Culture-based methods can help determine antibiotic susceptibility

### **Prevention**
- Consistent condom use
- Limiting sexual partners and regular STI screening
- Early detection and prompt treatment
