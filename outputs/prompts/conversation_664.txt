Assistant: ### **Management of Postpartum Depression**

The management of postpartum depression (PPD) typically requires a comprehensive approach, involving psychosocial support, therapy, and medication under close supervision. Interventions may vary based on individual needs, health history, and personal preferences.

---

### **1. Early Screening and Diagnosis**
- **Routine Screening**: Identifying risk factors early (e.g., personal or family history of depression, lack of support).
- **Professional Evaluation**: Use of standardized tools such as the Edinburgh Postnatal Depression Scale (EPDS).

---

### **2. Psychosocial Support**
**Key Elements:**
- **Counseling**: Individual, group, or family therapy sessions to foster a supportive environment.
- **Peer Support Groups**: Connecting with others who have experienced similar challenges.
- **Lifestyle Modifications**: Adequate rest, proper nutrition, and moderate exercise.

---

### **3. Pharmacotherapy**
- **Antidepressants**: SSRIs (e.g., Sertraline, Paroxetine) are often considered, with attention to breastfeeding status.
- **Hormonal Treatments**: In some cases, hormonal therapy may be explored under medical guidance.

---

### **4. Other Interventions**
- **Psychotherapy**: Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT), Interpersonal Therapy (IPT).
- **Brain Stimulation Therapies**: Such as Electroconvulsive Therapy (ECT), in severe cases.

---

### **Local Guidelines (Sri Lanka)**
- Refer to resources by the **Sri Lanka College of Psychiatrists** for locally adapted recommendations.
- Emphasis on culturally sensitive psychosocial interventions and close postpartum follow-up.

---

### **References**
1. American Psychiatric Association (APA) Guidelines for Treatment of Patients with Major Depressive Disorder.
2. WHO Recommendations on Maternal Mental Health.
3. National Institute of Mental Health (NIMH) resources on PPD.
